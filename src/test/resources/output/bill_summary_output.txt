The "RELIEF Act" (H.R. 815) aims to help families facing high childcare and education costs, particularly in low-income urban areas.

**Main Impact and Major Changes:**

The bill proposes creating a **new federal grant program** through the Department of Education. This program would provide **$50 million annually** from 2026 to 2030 (a total of $250 million) to **non-profit organizations**.

These non-profits would use the funds to offer **after-school tutoring and nutritional support** to K-12 students. To qualify, organizations must have been operating for at least three years, serve communities where at least 40% of residents live below the poverty line, and demonstrate measurable improvements in student academic achievement.
