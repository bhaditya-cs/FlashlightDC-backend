## H.R. 815 - The RELIEF Act: Boosting After-School Support for Students in Need

The **RELIEF Act** (Resources and Education for Liberty, Independence, and Empowerment for Families Act) aims to address the challenges families face with rising childcare and education costs, particularly in low-income urban areas.

**Main Impact & Changes:**

This bill proposes a new federal grant program to funnel money directly to community organizations that support K-12 students after school. It targets areas with significant financial hardship and focuses on both academic and nutritional needs.

**Key Provisions:**

*   **Grant Program for Non-profits:** The **Secretary of Education** will establish a new grant program.
    *   These grants will provide financial assistance to **non-profit organizations**.
    *   The purpose of the grants is to fund **after-school tutoring and nutritional support** for K-12 students.
*   **Targeted Communities:** To be eligible for a grant, organizations must serve a community where at least **40% of residents live below the federal poverty line**.
*   **Eligibility Requirements:** Eligible non-profits must have:
    *   Been in operation for at least **3 years**.
    *   The ability to demonstrate **measurable outcomes related to student academic improvement**.
*   **Funding:** The bill authorizes **$50,000,000** to be spent annually for fiscal years **2026 through 2030** to fund these grants.

**In essence, the RELIEF Act seeks to empower local non-profits in financially struggling communities to provide vital after-school programs, offering both academic help and healthy food to K-12 students.**
