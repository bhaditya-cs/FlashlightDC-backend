H.R. 815 - RELIEF Act

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the "Resources and Education for Liberty, Independence, and Empowerment for Families Act" or the "RELIEF Act".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.
Congress finds that many families struggle with the rising costs of childcare and primary education, particularly in low-income urban areas.

SEC. 3. GRANT PROGRAM.
(a) In General.—The Secretary of Education shall establish a grant program to provide financial assistance to non-profit organizations that offer after-school tutoring and nutritional support to K-12 students.
(b) Eligibility.—To be eligible for a grant under this section, an organization must—
    (1) have been in operation for at least 3 years;
    (2) serve a community where at least 40% of residents live below the federal poverty line; and
    (3) provide measurable outcomes related to student academic improvement.

SEC. 4. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.
There is authorized to be appropriated $50,000,000 for each of fiscal years 2026 through 2030 to carry out this Act.
